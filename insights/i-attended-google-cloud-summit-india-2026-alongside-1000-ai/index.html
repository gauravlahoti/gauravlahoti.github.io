I attended Google Cloud Summit India 2026 at Yashobhoomi, Delhi alongside 1000+ AI builders.

The keynote raised the bar. suchit puri, Prashanth Subrahmanyam and Abirami Sukumaran, Ph.D unpacked the Gemini Enterprise Agent Platform and shipped working agents on stage. Live, not slideware.

But the beat that stuck with me was a 1:1 conversation with Romin Irani on Antigravity 2.0 and ADK 2.0. We skipped past "what's new" and got into "why now": the rationale behind a shift this big and what it actually takes for builders to adapt instead of chasing every release. The developer's job is shifting from code-first to AI-first. You stop living in an editor and start directing the agent that writes the code. That is not a tooling update, it is a new way of working.

One session that stood out was a preview of an unreleased AI-powered architecture diagramming tool for Google Cloud. Stenal P Jolly pulled back the curtain on its multi-agent architecture. The premise is hard to argue with if you design systems for a living: we lose hours hand-drawing the same diagrams over and over. An agentic system that generates them is not a nice-to-have. It is overdue.

This year I was not there solo. A few of my Deloitte colleagues came along, Lakshay Sharma, Akshay Goel, SAJAL KULSHRESHTHA, and having people from your own team in the room changes the dynamic. Every session turned into a quick huddle on how we can actually apply this to our work.

Left with a full notebook and a short list I actually intend to act on.
